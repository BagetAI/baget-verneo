The Compliance Crisis

The 2026 Regulatory Squeeze on Investors

New French legislation has targeted property owners on multiple physical, fiscal, and administrative fronts. Operating traditionally is no longer a viable business option.

DPE Energy Bans

G-rated rental assets are already banned. F-rated units face an absolute, non-negotiable ban on January 1, 2028. Properties facing "thermal sieve" status are losing up to 30% of their open market valuation.

Impact: Immediate Banishment & Valuation Drop

Loi Le Meur Restrictions

Mandatory Declaloc national registration (deadline May 20, 2026). Tax abatements on standard short-term listings collapsed from 50% down to 30%. Building HOAs can now veto tourist lets via a 2/3 majority vote.

Impact: €10,000 Missing Registration Penalties

The Loi Hoguet Legal Trap

Standard conciergeries that transit guest money through their accounts without a physical "Carte G" license are operating illegally, risking criminal prosecution and automatic contract nullification.

Impact: €7,500 Criminal Fine & Arrest Risks
